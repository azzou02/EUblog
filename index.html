Day 4 (5/16) - La Caserne + Julie from Commitment

This was our first stop at La Caserne - Le Lab. I (Alison) found it really cool that we were able to see the different machineries used to help accelerate the process of prototyping. I also loved finally hearing someone talk about using CAD to help reduce waste and model clothes!
This was our second stop - Studio Paillette. It is a company that has a similar model to Rent the Runway, where their clients can rent different accessories and articles of clothing to help people act more sustainably. Moreover, they have collaborations with different brands, giving them another way to get rid of their deadstock in a more sustainable fashion.
This was our last stop - NONA Source. This company sells deadstock material of luxury brands to companies that can't afford or don't want a large batch of material. This is a super cool concept as not only is it sustainable, it also helps smaller brands get higher quality material for a lower price.
La Caserne! It actually used to be the first fire station at Paris.
A cute pastel-hued homes that Nadia recommended I check out. I love architecture that portray cozy, small town vibes (more commonly seen in Eastern France).
Europe is Europing! I love how active everyone is and living everyday as if it's the weekends. A discussion we had after visiting the fashion school was how education systems could affect people's perspective regarding ambition in life and "success."

Day 7 (5/19) - Fragonard Perfume Workshop

After watching the BCC video on perfume and child labor (Jasmine extraction), I've developed more distrust for these big name brands. From the perfume museum tour, I learned that the base coat oil used to be from animals to help with lingering of smell. Now, they've pivoted towards synthetic plant-based or chemically derived methods. I wonder if Jasmine smell could also be replicated. However, I believe that might lead to another flawed system of job scarcity as these Jasmine pickers won't be able to have anymore income sources. All that said, perfume making was really fun! I wish we had more options of smells because I wanted to make my own floral smell.
In the afternoon, Prof. Bloomquist, Ollie, and I (Michelle) went to to Roland Garros. I've never been invested in tennis, but after going there and being able to support Taiwan (Joanna Garland), I think I found a new sport that I must learn how to play. Once the rules made sense, I get the hype!
Dinner time!!! I (Alison) went to a foundu restaurant with Ava, Ava, and Bridget. It was a little adventure getting in as our reservation was cancelled, but we luckily got the last spot. The had wine and drinks served in a baby bottle which I found quite endearing.
Fragonard musuem tour and workshop was a really interesting expereince. I had no idea that the base note is what allows the perfume to stay longer on our skin, and that aluminum bottle extends the longevity of the perfume to ~8 years. I was also surprised to learn from the documentary how easy it is to find a loophole in the system to be able to present oneself as ethical practice, when there is child labor, and the different levels to processes to get a perfume to the market. Hopefully, the agency checking for safe practice can do better, and the companies that have these jasmine farms can pay a higher wage to the workers, so they won't have to bring their children to help with income. But overall, the museum and the process of making our own perfume was super fun.

Day 8 (5/20) - Palais Galliera Guided Visit + Guided Tour of Fashion District Mickael Cotte

Palais Galliera - The evolution of fashion is fascinating. The pieces that stood out to me were the swimsuits and the blue dress. Fun fact about the swimsuit was that for men it originally covered the top too because back in the 1910s, having pale skin was more fashionable. Then when sports become big, having tan skin meant "healthy" and active. The blue freeness of the dress marked a change in women's fashion. It diverges from the need for corset and thight forms.
Highlights of the fashion district tour was seeing the Emily In Paris scenes in person but also the different marketing backstories. Life experiences have a great impact in designer's products (e.g. the lid of Chanel No. 5 perfume bottle was inspired by the geometry of Place Vendome). Marketing concept (storyliving) - A big marketing movement that I need fashion brands should implement is cafes!
