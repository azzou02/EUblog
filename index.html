Day 7 (5/19) - Fragonard Perfume Workshop

After watching the BCC video on perfume and child labor (Jasmine extraction), I've developed more distrust for these big name brands. From the perfume museum tour, I learned that the base coat oil used to be from animals to help with lingering of smell. Now, they've pivoted towards synthetic plant-based or chemically derived methods. I wonder if Jasmine smell could also be replicated. However, I believe that might lead to another flawed system of job scarcity as these Jasmine pickers won't be able to have anymore income sources. All that said, perfume making was really fun! I wish we had more options of smells because I wanted to make my own floral smell.
In the afternoon, Prof. Bloomquist, Ollie, and I (Michelle) went to to Roland Garros. I've never been invested in tennis, but after going there and being able to support Taiwan (Joanna Garland), I think I found a new sport that I must learn how to play. Once the rules made sense, I get the hype!

Day 8 (5/20) - Palais Galliera Guided Visit + Guided Tour of Fashion District Mickael Cotte

Palais Galliera - The evolution of fashion is fascinating. The pieces that stood out to me were the swimsuits and the blue dress. Fun fact about the swimsuit was that for men it originally covered the top too because back in the 1910s, having pale skin was more fashionable. Then when sports become big, having tan skin meant "healthy" and active. The blue freeness of the dress marked a change in women's fashion. It diverges from the need for corset and thight forms.
Highlights of the fashion district tour was seeing the Emily In Paris scenes in person but also the different marketing backstories. Life experiences have a great impact in designer's products (e.g. the lid of Chanel No. 5 perfume bottle was inspired by the geometry of Place Vendome). Marketing concept (storyliving) - A big marketing movement that I need fashion brands should implement is cafes!
